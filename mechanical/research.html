Dr. Neha Sardana@Nano-structured devices and optical materials Lab

General:
Device design and development: Different fields of nanotechnology, EE, ME and/or MME come together for a device that can be used to provide sustainable solutions for day-to-day use or for the industry.
Core:
Prediction of device efficiency and material system stability using different software (Vasp-DFT, MatLAB, COMSOL, etc) with a view towards futuristic aerospace, high-temperature, and/or sensing applications.

Dr. Pratik Ray@IDEAs Lab (Interface Dynamics an Energetics of Alloys)

Our goal is to study and manipulate bulk thermodynamics as well as the interfaces in materials in order to engineer novel alloys with enhanced engineering properties.
Presently, we are working on developing Machine Learning enabled methods for investigating materials. Additionally, we will be building novel equipment for studying interfaces during solidification processing of materials.
